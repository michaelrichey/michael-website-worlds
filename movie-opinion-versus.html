My Magic Dog (1997)

Director: John Putch
Writer: Hamilton Underwood
Stars: Leo Milbrook, Bryan Mendez, Jessica Knoblauch

Synopsis: A boy's dog's ghost returns to help the boy win a custody battle, fight bullies, and find his loving but shy step-dad a nice girlfriend.

Perkbill's Opinion

Thumbs Down

My Magic Dog is another one of Hollywood's cash grab ghost dog movies, the first of which being Jim Jarmusch's independent film Ghost Dog, which kicked off the Ghost Dog craze.

I found the young boy petulant, the step-dad malodorous, and the dog insufferable at best. The subplot involving the Ghost Dog's carrying various trays through the living room to make it look like a ghost is haunting the place so yuppies won't buy the boy's step-dad's house was completely contrived and nonsensical. Why make it look like a ghost is haunting the house when a ghost is literally haunting the house? Absurd!

Crispy's Opinion

Thumbs UP!

My Magic Dog is a delight for the whole family. Even cynical teens and world-weary seniors can find something to love in this charming romp.

I found the young boy delightful, the step-dad filling me with delight, and the dog delight-giving-to-me. The subplot involving the Ghost Dog's using his x-ray vision to look at a lady dog's skeletons was delight-ridden. Why not have a dead dog becoming aroused by the bones of a living dog? Genius!
